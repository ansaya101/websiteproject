1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

The term IT is an abbreviation for Information Technology, “It refers to anything related to computing technology, such as networking, hardware, software, the Internet, or the people that work with these technologies.”[1] It essentially means that we are receiving and giving information via technology, and in certain careers (particularly those who work in an IT department) it becomes an essential part of their job.

Using IT can help a business grow through enabeling communication to a wider audience, produce systems to increase productivity and can be environmentally friendly by reducing the need for paper.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR stands for General Data Protection Regulation and is an EU regulation on data protection which applies to all members of the EU and EAU. “The aim of the GDPR is to protect all EU citizens from privacy and data breaches in today’s data-driven world.” [2] It affects the way data is stored, for example data protection by design and data protection by default ensures that the data is processed in a protective manner and that no unnecessary data is obtained. They also set out to ensure that the data is obtained with fully informed consent and be be accessed (right to access), modified (Data Portability) or withdrawn (right to be forgotten) by the ‘data subject’ Not only is data collection important but also the monitoring of it, in many big companies a Data Protection Officers will be enforced when using “processing operations which require regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale or of special categories of data or data relating to criminal convictions and offences.” [3] In any case of data breach the GDPR makes sure it is the responsibility of the company to report it, and in cases where there is a risk of harm the ‘data subject’ must be notified within 72 hours of first having become aware of the breach. The ‘Increased Territorial Scope (extraterritorial applicability)’ means that the GDPR has increased jurisdiction. This means that they are not limited by the location or size of a business and will fine those in breach of the ‘data subjects’ security. This impacts digital products because it puts a guideline on how to obtain and store data and impacts what data you can collect even if you a small business such as an independent person working from home or someone making a website/blog.
